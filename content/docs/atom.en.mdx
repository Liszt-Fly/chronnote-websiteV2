## 2. Core Logic: One Entity, Infinite References

In Chronnote, it is crucial to establish a key concept: **Entity vs. Reference**.

- **Atom Library (The Entity)**: Each Atom has one and only one "true self," which always resides in your **Atom Library**.
- **Workspace (The Reference)**: When you view an Atom in a whiteboard, folder, or document, what you are actually seeing is a "projection" or "reference" of it.
- **Sidebar Page View (The Reference)**: In the Page View sidebar, whether you create a new item directly or add it by dragging from the Atom Library, you are simply creating a reference.

This means you haven't copied the file; you have merely opened a "window" to it in a different location.

## 4. Synchronization: One Change, Updates Everywhere

The biggest advantage of Atoms is the synchronization mechanism.

After dragging a note onto the Whiteboard, you can modify the content directly within that card (e.g., changing the title or adding a paragraph).

## 5. More Scenarios: Page View

Besides free-form collage on the Whiteboard, Atoms fully support traditional hierarchical management.

In the **Page View** sidebar, you can organize your knowledge tree just like managing traditional folders. The logic remains the same:

1. Find the Atom you need in the Atom Library.
2. Drag it into a specific page (or folder) in the sidebar.
3. A new reference is formed instantly.

This way, you can associate it visually on a Whiteboard while also locating it quickly in a structured directory.

## Summary

- **Atoms are unique**: The "true entity" resides centrally in the Atom Library.
- **References are infinite**: They can appear anywhere (Whiteboards, Page Views, etc.).
- **Sync is real-time**: Editing any reference means editing the Atom itself.

Once you understand the **"Atom + Reference"** model, you have mastered the core mindset of Chronnote. Whether you are building a knowledge system or planning a project, you can expand endlessly upon this unified logic.
